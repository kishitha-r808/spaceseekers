Solar wind

What is Solar Wind?

The sun is blasting a barrage of charged particles (electrons, protons, and other ions) out into the solar system. We call this ongoing stream of material the solar wind. These materials are released from the upper atmosphere of the Sun, known as the corona. These particles zoom through space at speeds between 300 and 800 km/s, interacting with the magnetic fields and atmospheres of planets.

Advantages of Solar Wind

  • Helps create auroras (Northern & Southern Lights).
  • Shapes planetary magnetospheres.
  • Provides info about solar activity & space weather.
  • Potential for solar sail propulsion in space travel.

Disadvantages of Solar Wind

  • Can disrupt satellites & communications.
  • Causes radiation hazards for astronauts.
  • Damages spacecraft electronics over time.

Damages caused by Solar Wind:

Economical Damage:

  • Disruption of power grids (blackouts).
  • Loss of communication and GPS navigation signals.
  • Costly damage to satellites and spacecraft instruments.
